What we want

Go Fetch Fido provides timely, medically informed response and safe, climate-controlled transport for urgent canine cases in Ballard—so dogs reach the right ER faster and owners aren’t forced into unsafe DIY transport.

The problem

  • Time-sensitive conditions (trauma, bleeding, respiratory distress, toxin ingestion) require fast stabilization + transport.
  • Owners aren’t equipped (training, oxygen, restraint/secure vehicle setup) to move an injured dog safely.
  • Access friction: Ballard clinics have limited hours; Seattle emergency hospitals are centralized and busy (triage/first-come delays).
  • Panic + logistics slow decision-making and worsen outcomes.

What goes wrong today

Delays

⏱ ~30–60 min

Owners search for an open clinic, coordinate transport, and arrive without pre-coordination.

Unsafe transport

⏱ ~10–20 min

Unrestrained dogs, poor temperature control, no monitoring, and risky lifting/handling.

Suboptimal stabilization

⏱ ~5–15 min

Bleeding control, oxygen, and basic life support often happen late or not at all.

Fragmented handoffs

⏱ ~10–25 min

Receiving ER isn’t prepped; triage slows; information is incomplete.

How it works

  1. Dispatch: owner calls/texts; guided triage + destination selection.
  2. Arrive + stabilize: rapid assessment, oxygen/bleeding control, safe packaging.
  3. Transport: monitoring, updates to owner, ETA to clinic.
  4. Handoff: structured report + transfer of care to receiving team.
  5. Follow-up: documentation + referral loop with partner clinics.

Why now

  • More urban dog ownership + smaller households → fewer “backup drivers” in emergencies.
  • Capacity constraints in emergency hospitals make coordinated arrivals and transport more valuable.
  • Trust + operations: modern dispatch, GPS ETAs, and digital handoffs make neighborhood response feasible.

Addressable dogs in Ballard: ~5,000–8,000 (scenario range).
